SLIDE 3: Data Sources Analyzed
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Three Contenders
1. Yahoo Finance (via yfinance)
   - Target: Retail investors & prototyping.
   - Pros: Free, real-time, easy access.
   - Cons: Black-box adjustments, survivorship bias risk.
2. CRSP (Center for Research in Security Prices)
   - Target: Academic research (Gold Standard).
   - Pros: Permanent identifiers (PERMNO), meticulous delisting data.
   - Cons: Delayed data, hard to access (WRDS).
3. Compustat - Capital IQ
   - Target: Fundamental analysis.
   - Pros: Detailed balance sheet/income statement data.
   - Cons: No direct price/return data (must be linked).

[Speaker Notes]
- We looked at Yahoo for the "retail" view.
- CRSP is the academic benchmark for stock prices.
- Compustat is the standard for fundamental data like earnings and debt.

SLIDE 5: Part II - Historical Integrity (Ticker Changes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Ticker Trap: Case Study META
Key Points:
- Problem: Tickers are reused. They are NOT permanent identifiers.
- Case Study: Facebook (FB) -> Meta Platforms (META).
- Yahoo Finance: Querying "FB" returns only recent data (118 days). Querying "META" returns full history.
    - Risk: Discontinuous datasets if you don't know the new ticker.
- CRSP Solution: Uses PERMNO (13407).
    - Links FB and META seamlessly as one security.

Suggested Visual: FE511_Project/section_3_4_identifiers.png

[Speaker Notes]
- We found a critical flaw in ticker-based analysis.
- If you query "FB" in Yahoo today, you miss 10 years of Facebook history.
- CRSP's PERMNO system prevents this error entirely.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 6: Part II - Survivorship Bias (Delistings)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Survivorship Bias: The Lehman Brothers Lesson
Key Points:
- Survivorship Bias: Ignoring failed companies leads to overstated returns.
- Case Study: Lehman Brothers (LEH) Bankruptcy (2008).
- Yahoo Finance: No historical data available. The -100% loss is effectively erased from history.
- CRSP:
    - Captures the final delisting return (DLRET) of -60% on the final day.
    - Accurately records the total loss for investors.
- Impact: Backtests excluding Lehman overstate Financial Sector returns by ~5-15% during crises.

Suggested Visual: FE511_Project/survivorship_bias_providers.csv (screenshot as table) OR FE511_Project/section_3_2_quality_dashboard.png

[Speaker Notes]
- This is the most dangerous error.
- If you backtest a bank strategy in Yahoo, Lehman Brothers never existed.
- Your strategy looks amazing because you only kept the winners. CRSP forces you to face the reality of the losses.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 7: Part III - Price Adjustments
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The Mathematics of Returns: Adjustments
Key Points:
- Raw Prices vs. Adjusted Prices.
- Corporate Actions: Stock Splits (Apple 4:1) and Dividends.
- Yahoo Approach: "Black Box" Adjusted Close. Adjusts backward blindly.
- CRSP Approach: Explicit Factors (CFACPR).
    - Allows user to verify and choose adjustment methodology.
    - Separates Price Returns (RETX) from Total Returns (RET).

Suggested Visual: FE511_Project/section_3_3_stock_split.png OR FE511_Project/section_3_3_dividends.png

[Speaker Notes]
- A 4-for-1 split isn't a 75% crash.
- Both providers handle this, but CRSP gives you the mathematical factors to verify it. Yahoo just gives you a changed number.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 8: Part IV - Entity Identification
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The "Master Key" Problem
Key Points:
- How do we link Price (CRSP) to Fundamentals (Compustat)?
- Tickers fail (Reused). Names fail (Changed).
- Solution: CUSIP or CCM (CRSP-Compustat Merged) Link Table.
- Our Analysis:
    - Mapped CRSP PERMNOs to Compustat GVKEYs.
    - Essential for calculating ratios like P/E (Price from CRSP / Earnings from Compustat).

Suggested Visual: FE511_Project/section_3_4_identifiers.png

[Speaker Notes]
- You cannot do serious fundamental analysis without linking databases.
- We used the CCM link table to bridge the gap between market data and accounting data.

SLIDE 10: The Shocking Divergence (Results)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: The 115% Difference
Key Points:
- Yahoo Finance Result: 274.12% Total Return ($374k Final Value).
- CRSP Result: 158.69% Total Return ($258k Final Value).
- Difference: 115 percentage points!
- Why?
    - Accumulated small differences in dividend adjustments.
    - Differences in total return calculation methodologies over 10 years.
    - Compound impact of "small" data discrepancies.

Suggested Visual: FE511_Project/part4_portfolio_performance.png OR FE511_Project/strategy_backtest_comparison.png

[Speaker Notes]
- This is the slide that matters.
- Yahoo data doubled the perceived performance compared to the rigorous academic data.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE 12: Conclusion & Recommendations
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Title: Final Verdict
Key Points:
- For Retail/Trading: Yahoo Finance is fine for real-time monitoring and short-term execution.
- For Backtesting/Research: CRSP is MANDATORY.
    - Must handle delistings (Survivorship Bias).
    - Must use permanent identifiers.
- For Fundamentals: Compustat is unrivaled but needs careful linking.
- Final Word: Know your data source. It determines your results.

Suggested Visual: FE511_Project/section_3_1_feature_heatmap.png (Recap)

[Speaker Notes]
- Don't use Yahoo for historical backtesting.
- Use CRSP for truth, Compustat for depth, and Yahoo for speed.
